History

To whom it may concern,

I was born on June 3, 1995 in New Providence, Bahamas. Raised by focused, hard working, and honest parents, I grew up blessed with the opportunity to gain an elementary and highschool education. While going through highschool, I had opportunities to work and emulate the good habits and traits I saw in my parents while also developing traits such a integrity, sacrifice, empathy, and teamwork.

After graduating highschool, I was humbled by an opportunity to spend two years as a Full-time Volunteer for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints in Jamaica. Spending a vast majority of that time with people of various backgrounds and socio-economic status to assist them and focus on their needs helped me to develop and polish various interpersonal and leadership skills such as communication, emotional intelligence, public speaking, team management, record keeping, and punctuality.

While in Jamaica, I decided on attending college at Brigham Young University-Idaho. Upon returning home, I filled out an application, was accepted, and began studying as a Computer Engineering Major. While attending BYU-I, I have encountered various physical, mental, emotional and financial set backs, which has led me to find my true path, passion, and purpose as a Software Engineering Major working towards becoming a Web & Mobile Developer.

Although there were many challenges, my time in college has also helped me to find personal growth while also experiencing some of the happiest moments of my life. I have had the opportunity to gain valuable experience and skills as an individual and in a team in classes and at work. I was able to meet the love of my life and marry her in April of 2021. And, I learned the importance of interdependance by reaching out to various faculty and academic advisors for mentoring.

In this moment, I am not sure what the future holds. However, I remain optimistic of my ability to achieve anything I set my mind to do. I am searching for internships, working on personal projects, and nurturing my home and family life.

Sincerely,

Joshua A. Dean

Mindset

Mindset drives and shapes all that we do—how we engage with others and how we behave in every moment and situation.

-The Arbinger Institute, The Outward Mindset: Seeing Beyond Ourselves

I have dedicated a great portion of my life to changing and refining my mindset. Life is filled with challenges and obstacles in the form of people and events. I have learned for myself that how I view these obstacles and challenges and how I choose to approach them can be altered and improved upon through first awareness, second practice, and finally personal accountability and reflection.

I believe in and am consistently trying to emulate an OUTWARD mindset as described by the Arbringer Institute in their book, The Outward Mindset: Seeing Beyond Ourselves. If you ever have the time, give it a read. You can find it on Amazon for 10 - 20 dollars. The Outward Mindset is exemplified by an ability to see the needs, objectives, and challenges of those around you, adjusting your efforts to help meet those needs, and measuring your impact. I feel greater satisfaction at home, work, and socially when I'm honestly helping others to meet their needs. In the process of helping others, I often find solutions to solving my own challenges.
